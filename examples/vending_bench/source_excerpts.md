# Key Excerpts from "Vending-bench implementation.pdf"

- "Vending-Bench provides a sandboxed business simulation where an agent must run a vending-machine company for hundreds of simulated days." (Introduction)
- "Agents research products, negotiate with suppliers, manage inventory, set prices, collect cash and pay fees across more than 20 million tokens." (Introduction)
- "Each run ends after 2000 messages or earlier if the business goes bankrupt." (Introduction)
- "The benchmark reports net worth, units sold and tool-usage statistics to evaluate economic performance and decision quality." (Introduction)
- "Implement a simulator, expose Inspect tools, and build a supervisor plus sub-agent architecture for long-horizon control." (Introduction)
- "The simulator should be deterministic given a random seed." (Non-functional requirements)
- "Use seeded pseudo-random number generators for demand noise and supplier lead times." (Non-functional requirements)
- "Each agent action (tool call) advances the simulation clock by 5 min-5 h. Agents may skip to the next day with a wait_for_next_day tool." (Functional requirements)
- "Provide scratchpad, key-value store and vector database to persist notes and state." (Functional requirements)
- "Research: The agent uses the ai_web_search tool to find popular vending machine products and supplier contact information." (Supplier model)
- "When the agent sends purchase orders they are added to orders with a random delivery time and payment is deducted immediately." (Daily cycle)
- "The simulator maintains cash_balance, storage_inventory, machine_inventory, orders, email_inbox/outbox, demand_params and random_seed." (State representation)
- "Simulate customer purchases using the demand model, update machine_inventory and cash_in_machine, and charge the $2 fee from cash_balance." (Daily cycle)
- "The environment sends a morning email summarizing sales, deliveries, cash balance, cash in machine and inventories." (Daily cycle)
- "Use GPT-4o to generate reference price, price elasticity and base daily sales at order time." (Demand model)
- "Apply day-of-week, month/season and weather modifiers, and a variety penalty up to a 50% reduction." (Demand model)
- "Add random noise, round demand to integers and cap by available quantity." (Demand model)
- "The sub-agent operates restock_machine, set_prices, collect_cash and get_machine_inventory to handle physical actions." (Physical tools)
- "The sub-agent is an Inspect ReAct agent with its own prompt and memory for multi-step actions." (Sub-agent design)
- "Use Pydantic models to validate tool parameters, forbid unknown fields, and log each tool event with per-tool message and token limits." (Tool design considerations)
- "Write daily summaries to the scratchpad covering sales, inventory, cash balance, outstanding orders and upcoming deliveries." (Memory strategies)
- "Store structured state in the key-value store and embeddings of emails in the vector database for retrieval." (Memory strategies)
- "Use summarisation to keep the scratchpad manageable while preserving important facts." (Memory strategies)
- "Maintain a cash buffer, reorder when days-of-stock drops below five, and adjust prices within elasticity-driven bounds." (Balancing inventory and cash flow)
- "Set sub-agent handoff limits around 8-24 messages and use quarantine filters to strip tool chatter." (Sub-agent delegation)
- "Build the supervisor with environment tools, memory tools and transfer_to_vending; run_agent enforces time, message and token limits." (Agent build & evaluation)
